The anatomical illustration presents the bones of the elbow in ninety-degree flexion with numbered labels identifying structural contours, joint surfaces, and anatomical landmarks from lateral (1 to 5) and medial (6 to 12) perspectives. Label 1 marks the lateral epicondyle of the humerus. Label 2 identifies the capitulum. Label 3 shows the neck of the radius. Label 4 indicates the radius. Label 5 marks the ulna. Label 6 identifies the humerus. Label 7 shows the head of the radius. Label 8 marks the radial tuberosity. Label 9 identifies the coronoid process of the ulna. Label 10 shows the trochlear notch. Label 11 marks the groove for the ulnar nerve on the humerus. Label 12 identifies the medial epicondyle of the humerus.
