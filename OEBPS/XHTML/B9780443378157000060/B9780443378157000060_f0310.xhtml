The anatomical illustration presents an anterior view of the upper limb with nine labeled regions indicating cutaneous innervation. Label 1 identifies the supraclavicular nerves originating from the cervical plexus at spinal roots C 3 and C 4. Label 2 marks the axillary nerve and the superior lateral brachial cutaneous nerve from C 5 and C 6. Label 3 indicates the radial nerve and the inferior lateral brachial cutaneous nerve from C 5 and C 6. Label 4 shows the lateral antebrachial cutaneous nerve derived from the musculocutaneous nerve at C 5 and C 6. Label 5 denotes the superficial branch of the radial nerve from C 6 through C 8. Label 6 highlights the palmar and digital branches of the median nerve from C 6 through C 8. Label 7 refers to the palmar and digital branches of the ulnar nerve from C 8 through T 1. Label 8 points to the medial antebrachial cutaneous nerve from C 8 through T 1. Label 9 identifies the medial brachial cutaneous nerve from C 8 through T 1 and the intercostobrachial cutaneous nerve from T 2.
