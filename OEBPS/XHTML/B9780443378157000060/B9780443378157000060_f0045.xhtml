The anatomical illustration presents the bones of the forearm with numbered labels identifying structural features and connective elements that support articulation and muscle attachment. The anterior view of the Right radius and ulna in supination is displayed on the left. The anterior view of the Right radius and ulna in pronation is on the right. Label 1 marks the head. Label 2 identifies the neck. Label 3 shows the radial tuberosity. Label 4 indicates the interosseous membrane of the forearm. Label 5 marks the ulnar styloid process. Label 6 identifies the oblique cord. Label 7 shows the tuberosity of the ulna. Label 8 marks the trochlear notch. Label 9 identifies the olecranon. Label 10 shows the radial styloid process. Label 11 marks the dorsal radial tubercle, also called the tubercle of Lister. Label 12 identifies the posterior border.
