The anatomical illustration presents an anterior view of the shoulder and arm with ten labeled structures. Label 1 identifies the medial, intermediate, and lateral supraclavicular nerves originating from the cervical plexus. Label 2 marks the medial brachial cutaneous nerve. Label 3 indicates the superior lateral brachial cutaneous nerve derived from the axillary nerve. Label 4 shows the cephalic vein. Label 5 denotes the median antebrachial vein. Label 6 repeats the cephalic vein. Label 7 highlights the basilic vein. Label 8 refers to the median cubital vein. Label 9 points to the lateral antebrachial cutaneous nerve, which is the terminal part of the musculocutaneous nerve. Label 10 repeats the basilic vein.
