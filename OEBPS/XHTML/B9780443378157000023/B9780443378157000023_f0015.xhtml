The anatomical illustration shows cervical vertebrae with numbered labels in two panels. The top panel presents a superior view of a single vertebra with labels 1 to 4, and 9 and 10. The bottom panel presents a lateral view, with labels 5 to 8. Label 1 indicates the vertebral body. Label 2 points to the transverse process. Label 3 identifies the pedicle. Label 4 shows the lamina. Label 5 indicates the dens of the axis. Label 6 points to the vertebral body of C 4. Label 7 identifies the spinous process of C 7, also known as the vertebra prominens. Label 8 shows the spinous processes of C 2 and C 3. Label 9 indicates the superior articular process of C 4. Label 10 points to the transverse foramen.
