The anatomical illustration shows the arterial supply of the spinal cord in two panels. The upper panel, labeled section through thoracic level in an anterosuperior view, shows the branching pattern of arteries around the vertebral column and spinal cord, with labels 1 to 6. The lower panel provides a schematic view of the same circulation, highlighting the arterial distribution, showing labels 7 to 9. Label 1 indicates the posterior spinal arteries. Label 2 points to the anterior spinal artery. Label 3 identifies the anterior radicular artery. Label 4 shows the dorsal branch of the posterior intercostal artery. Label 5 indicates the posterior intercostal artery. Label 6 points to the thoracic descending aorta. Label 7 identifies the segmental medullary artery. Label 8 shows another view of the anterior spinal artery. Label 9 indicates the pial arterial plexus. Label 10 points to the right posterior spinal artery.
