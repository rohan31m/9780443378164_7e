The anatomical diagram shows vertebral bones from three different perspectives with numbered labels. The top left panel shows a superior view with labels 1 to 3. The middle panel shows a lateral view with labels 7 to 11. The bottom panel also presents a lateral view with labels 4 to 6. Label 1 indicates the vertebral foramen. Label 2 points to the lamina. Label 3 identifies the pedicle. Label 4 shows the vertebral body. Label 5 indicates the inferior articular process and facet. Label 6 points to the spinous process. Label 7 identifies the inferior vertebral notch. Label 8 shows the inferior costal facet, which is used for rib articulation. Label 9 indicates the transverse costal facet, also involved in rib articulation. Label 10 points to the superior costal facet. Label 11 identifies the superior articular process and facet.
