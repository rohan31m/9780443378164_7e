The two-part anatomical illustration is divided into an upper view of the jejunum and a lower view of the ileum. In the upper section representing the jejunum, 1 depicts looping vessels forming a few simple arches. 2 shows small straight branches extending toward the intestinal wall. 3 represents the supportive layer beneath the inner lining. 4 indicates the soft inner surface responsible for absorption. 5 marks a series of prominent circular ridges along the inner wall. 6 shows the thin tissue fold that attaches the intestine to the posterior wall. In the lower section representing the ileum, 7 depicts a more complex network of arterial loops. 8 indicates the thin outer covering layer. 9 marks the outer muscle layer running lengthwise. 10 shows the inner muscle layer arranged in rings. 11 represents clusters of oval immune nodules along the inner wall.
