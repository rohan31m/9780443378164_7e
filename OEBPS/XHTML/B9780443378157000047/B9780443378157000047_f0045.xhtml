The illustration presents the posterior aspect of the diaphragm with its major openings and attachments. 1 shows the dome-shaped muscle that separates the chest cavity from the abdominal cavity. 2 identifies the muscular column on the left side anchoring the diaphragm to the spine. 3 marks the main artery cut at the point where it passes through the diaphragm, and 4 shows the thin lymphatic channel, also cut, that normally travels alongside the artery through the same region.
