The anterior illustration of the lower chest and abdomen depicts several key structures labeled 1 to 9. 1 shows the broad sheet of outer abdominal muscle, which has a muscular section at the sides and a thin tendinous portion toward the front midline. 2 marks the saw-like muscle of the chest wall attaching to the ribs. 3 indicates the wide back muscle that wraps forward toward the side of the body. 4 highlights the central fibrous band where both sides of the abdomen meet. 5 represents the outer fatty layer of tissue beneath the skin. 6 points to the deeper, thinner membranous layer lying just above the muscle. 7 identifies the delicate sheath surrounding the cord-like structure extending toward the groin. 8 marks a small oval opening through which the cord passes near the groin area. 9 denotes the curved ligament stretching between the hip and the pubic bone.
