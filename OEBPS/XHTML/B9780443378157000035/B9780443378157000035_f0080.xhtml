The posterior illustration of the thoracic and upper abdominal veins presents the drainage system of the chest wall and abdominal organs. 1 A large vein on the right side collects blood from the head and arm. 2 It joins below to form a thick vertical vessel descending toward the heart. 3 Along its right side, a curved vein ascends from the lower chest to join it. 4 A large vessel from below the diaphragm is seen cut at its upper end. 5 Near the stomach, a vein runs along the lesser curvature. 6 Small branches from the food pipe drain into this same vein. 7 From the small intestine region, a major vein ascends upward. 8 Behind the stomach, a large horizontal vein runs toward the midline. 9 On the left side of the chest, a vertical vessel ascends to join the azygos system. 10 Above it, another smaller parallel channel drains the upper intercostal spaces. 11 Narrow veins run between the ribs to empty into these longitudinal vessels. 12 On the upper left side, a large transverse vein crosses to join the one on the right. 13 Behind the esophagus, a thin duct ascends vertically carrying lymph toward the base of the neck.
