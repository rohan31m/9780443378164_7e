The anterior illustration of the human thorax presents the surface projection of the lungs, pleura, and superior mediastinal structures within the rib cage.1 A vertical air passage runs down the midline of the neck into the chest. 2 On either side, the pleural covering extends upward into the lower neck. 3 A curved bone crosses the upper chest just above the first rib. 4 The topmost rib and its front cartilage form the boundary of the chest cavity. 5 On the right lung, a horizontal groove separates the upper and middle parts. 6 A slanting groove divides the middle and lower portions. 7 At the lower edge of the chest, a narrow space marks the junction between the ribs and diaphragm. 8 Below the right lung, a small pear-shaped organ lies beneath the liver. 9 The large dark organ occupies most of the upper right abdomen. 10 To the left, a hollow organ sits just below the diaphragm. 11 Further left, a soft oval organ rests near the back of the upper abdomen. 12 On the left lung, a slanting groove divides the upper and lower lobes. 13 A smooth, curved margin outlines the left side of the heart. 14 Near this margin, a deep notch is seen on the inner border of the left lung. 15 Above it, a curved arching vessel passes over the heart. 16 The pointed upper tip of the lung extends into the lower neck region.
