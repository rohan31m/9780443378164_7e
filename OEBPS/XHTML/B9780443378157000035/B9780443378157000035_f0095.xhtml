The anterior illustration of the opened thoracic cavity presents the lungs, pleura, diaphragm, and structures in the superior mediastinum. 1 The thin lining of the chest wall is partly removed to show the lungs beneath. 2 On the right side, the lung is divided into upper, middle, and lower portions. 3 A slanting groove separates the middle and lower parts of the right lung. 4 Below both lungs, the pleural covering extends over the diaphragm. 5 On the left side, a tongue-like projection extends forward near the heart. 6 A deep groove separates the upper and lower parts of the left lung. 7 The left lung shows two main sections, upper and lower. 8 Near the front border of the left lung, a curved indentation marks the area close to the heart. 9 Along the inner chest wall, paired blood vessels run vertically toward the diaphragm. 10 Beside them, a thin nerve descends between the heart and lung toward the diaphragm. 11 In the neck, a large vein runs downward beside the windpipe. 12 Just above it, a small gland rests across the front of the neck below the voice box.
