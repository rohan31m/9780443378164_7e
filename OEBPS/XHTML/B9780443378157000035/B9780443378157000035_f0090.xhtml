The anterior illustration of the female chest wall and mammary gland presents the superficial and deep vasculature and the lymphatic drainage routes. 1 A large vein runs vertically along the side of the neck, draining blood from the head toward the chest. 2 Just below it, a short duct carries lymph from the right upper body and empties into the large vein. 3 High in the armpit, a group of small lymph nodes is seen near the topmost region. 4 Slightly lower, another group of nodes lies in the central part of the armpit. 5 Along the back wall of the armpit, a chain of nodes drains the posterior shoulder and upper back. 6 On the outer side of the armpit, another line of nodes receives lymph from the upper limb. 7 A small chest muscle is shown deep within the armpit region, partly covering some of the nodes. 8 Between the two chest muscles, a few small nodes lie in the space separating them. 9 Along the front wall of the armpit, a group of nodes collects lymph from the front of the chest and breast. 10 The large surface chest muscle covers most of the front wall. 11 Alongside the breastbone, a vertical chain of nodes runs close to the inner chest wall. 12 Small nodes near the breast tissue receive lymph directly from nearby glandular areas before draining into deeper groups.
