The anterior illustration of the opened thoracic cavity presents the internal aspects of the rib cage, musculature, and associated vasculature. 1 shows the layer of muscles lying between the ribs, running upward and backward, which help pull the ribs downward during exhalation. 2 marks a thin muscle on the inner front of the chest that spreads from the lower part of the breastbone to several ribs, helping lower the ribs during breathing. 3 shows the broad dome-shaped muscle forming the floor of the chest cavity that separates it from the abdomen and moves up and down during breathing. 4 represents small side branches of the main intercostal nerves, arteries, and veins running along the lower edges of the ribs to supply nearby muscles and tissues. 5 shows the main arteries, veins, and nerves running between the ribs toward the front of the chest wall. 6 marks the major artery and its paired veins running vertically along the inner front of the chest near the breastbone, providing blood supply to the chest wall. 7 shows the large blood vessels passing beneath the collarbone that carry blood to and from the upper chest and arms. 8 indicates the narrow muscle at the side of the neck that helps raise the first rib during deep breathing and lies close to these major vessels.
