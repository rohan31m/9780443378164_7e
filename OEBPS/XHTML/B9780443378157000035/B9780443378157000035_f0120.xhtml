The right lateral illustration presents the inner view of the opened right atrium of the heart with key internal and surrounding structures identified. 1 marks the large vein entering from the upper body, opening into the upper part of the chamber. 2 shows the nearby artery carrying blood toward the right lung. 3 and 4 indicate the upper and lower veins returning oxygenated blood from the right lung. 5 highlights the thin wall separating the right and left atria. 6 points to a shallow depression on this wall, a remnant of fetal circulation. 7 marks the large lower vein that brings blood from the lower body. 8 shows a small crescent-shaped fold guarding the entrance of this lower vein. 9 identifies the small opening where a vein from the heart wall itself drains into the atrium. 10 shows ridged muscular bands that line part of the atrial wall. 11 marks the inner flap of tissue that helps close the passage between the right atrium and right ventricle. 12 identifies the ridge dividing the smooth and ridged portions of the atrial wall. 13 points to the ear-like muscular pouch extending from the main chamber. 14 marks the main vessel carrying blood from the right ventricle to the lungs, and 15 shows the large upward vessel sending oxygen-rich blood from the left ventricle to the rest of the body.
