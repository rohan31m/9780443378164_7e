The anatomical illustration displays the anterior, middle, and posterior cranial fossae on the left side of the skull base with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the cribriform foramina, which transmit the olfactory nerves. Label 2 points to the optic canal, carrying the optic nerve or C N 2 and the ophthalmic artery. Label 3 identifies the superior orbital fissure, transmitting the oculomotor nerve or C N 3, trochlear nerve or C N 4, ophthalmic nerve or C N V 1, abducens nerve or C N 6, and the superior ophthalmic vein. Label 4 shows the foramen rotundum, which allows passage of the maxillary nerve or C N V 2, while label 5 indicates the foramen ovale, transmitting the mandibular nerve or C N V 3, accessory meningeal artery, and lesser petrosal nerve. Label 6 points to the foramen spinosum, for the middle meningeal artery and vein, and the meningeal branch of the mandibular nerve. Label 7 identifies the foramen lacerum, across which the greater petrosal nerve passes. Label 8 shows the carotid canal, transmitting the internal carotid artery and the internal carotid nerve plexus. Label 9 indicates the hiatus for the greater petrosal nerve. Label 10 points to the internal acoustic meatus, which carries the facial nerve or C N 7, vestibulocochlear nerve or C N 8, and the labyrinthine artery. Label 11 identifies the foramen magnum, transmitting the medulla oblongata, vertebral arteries, meningeal branches, venous plexus, and spinal roots of the accessory nerves. Label 12 shows the hypoglossal canal, for the hypoglossal nerve or C N 12. Label 13 indicates the jugular foramen, transmitting the inferior petrosal sinus, glossopharyngeal nerve or C N 9, vagus nerve or C N 10, accessory nerve or C N 11, sigmoid sinus, and posterior meningeal artery. Label 14 points to the posterior ethmoid foramen, carrying the posterior ethmoid artery, vein, and nerve. Label 15 identifies the foramen cecum, transmitting an emissary vein to the superior sagittal sinus.
