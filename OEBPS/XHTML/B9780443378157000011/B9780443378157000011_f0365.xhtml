The anatomical cross-section of the human ear shows a frontal view of the external, middle, and inner ear structures with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the incus. Label 2 points to the head of the malleus. Label 3 identifies the tympanic membrane or eardrum. Label 4 shows the cochlear or round window. Label 5 indicates the auditory or eustachian tube. Label 6 points to the cochlea. Label 7 identifies the vestibulocochlear nerve or cranial nerve 8. Label 8 shows the semicircular ducts, ampullae, utricle, and saccule. Finally, label 9 indicates the stapes in the vestibular or oval window.
