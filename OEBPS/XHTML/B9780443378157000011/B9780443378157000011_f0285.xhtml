The anatomical illustration of the pharyngeal and associated nerve structures shows a sagittal view of the head and neck region detailing the pathways of several cranial nerves and associated ganglia with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the geniculate ganglion. Label 2 points to the greater petrosal nerve. Label 3 identifies the deep petrosal nerve. Label 4 shows the lesser petrosal nerve. Label 5 indicates the otic ganglion. Label 6 points to the auriculotemporal nerve. Label 7 identifies the parotid gland. Label 8 shows the stylopharyngeus muscle along with its nerve branch from the glossopharyngeal nerve. Label 9 indicates the pharyngeal plexus, and Label 10 points to the carotid sinus nerve of Hering. Label 11 identifies the superior cervical ganglion. Label 12 shows the vagus nerve or cranial nerve ten. Label 13 indicates the jugular foramen. Label 14 points to the glossopharyngeal nerve or cranial nerve 9. Finally, label 15 identifies the inferior salivatory nucleus.
