The anatomy illustration presents a cross-section of the anterior segment of the human eye, showing the structures involved in aqueous humor outflow and accommodation with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the cornea. Label 2 points to the trabecular reticulum, which includes the spaces of Fontana. Label 3 identifies the scleral sinus or the canal of Schlemm. Label 4 shows the sclera. Label 5 indicates the ciliary body. Label 6 points to the ciliary muscle, which contains meridional and circular fibers. Label 7 identifies the zonular fibers or suspensory ligaments of the lens. Label 8 shows the posterior chamber. Label 9 indicates the dilator pupillae. Label 10 points to the sphincter pupillae. Label 11 identifies the lens. Label 12 shows the folds of the iris. Finally, label 13 indicates the anterior chamber.
