The anatomical illustration presents the trigeminal nerve with its branches across three illustrations with numbered labels. The top panel displays the maxillary and mandibular nerves and their branches as they enter the upper and lower jaws to supply the teeth and oral cavity, with labels 1 to 4 and 8 to 12. The inferior alveolar nerve and the anterior dental branches of the inferior alveolar nerve are labeled in the illustration. The middle panel shows the distribution of the dental nerves in the upper and lower jaws, with labels 5 to 7. The bottom panel focuses on the tongue, showing sensory areas for taste and touch, with labels 13 to 16. Label 1 indicates the ophthalmic nerve or cranial nerve V 1. Label 2 points to the trigeminal nerve or cranial nerve 5. Label 3 identifies the maxillary nerve or cranial nerve V 2. Label 4 shows the mandibular nerve or cranial nerve V 3. Label 5 indicates the anterior superior alveolar nerve. Label 6 points to the middle superior alveolar nerve. Label 7 identifies the posterior superior alveolar nerve and buccal nerve. Label 8 shows the lingual nerve. Label 9 indicates the inferior alveolar nerve. Label 10 points to the middle superior alveolar nerve. Label 11 identifies the anterior superior alveolar nerve. Label 12 shows the posterior superior alveolar nerve. Label 13 indicates the vagus nerve or cranial nerve 10, responsible for taste and general sensation via the superior laryngeal nerve. Label 14 points to the glossopharyngeal nerve or cranial nerve 9, which provides taste and general sensation through its lingual branches. Label 15 identifies the facial nerve or cranial nerve 7, which carries taste sensation via the chorda. Label 16 shows the trigeminal nerve or cranial nerve V, responsible for general sensation through V 3, its lingual nerve.
