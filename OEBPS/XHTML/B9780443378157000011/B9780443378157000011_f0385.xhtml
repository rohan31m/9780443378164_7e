The anatomical posterior view of the thyroid gland, larynx, pharynx, and associated vessels and nerves shows the neck region with numbered labels for the different glands, arteries, and veins. Label 1 indicates the superior laryngeal nerve. Label 2 points to its internal branch. Label 3 identifies the external branch of the superior laryngeal nerve. Label 4 shows the superior thyroid artery. Label 5 indicates the superior parathyroid gland. Label 6 points to the inferior parathyroid gland. Label 7 identifies the vagus nerve. Label 8 shows the subclavian artery. Label 9 indicates the thyrocervical trunk. Label 10 points to the recurrent laryngeal nerve. Label 11 identifies the inferior thyroid artery. Label 12 shows the right lobe of the thyroid gland. Label 13 indicates the cricopharyngeus muscle, which is part of the inferior pharyngeal constrictor. Label 14 points to the inferior pharyngeal constrictor itself. Label 15 identifies another branch of the vagus nerve. Label 16 shows the epiglottis. Label 17 indicates the internal carotid artery. Label 18 points to the external carotid artery.
