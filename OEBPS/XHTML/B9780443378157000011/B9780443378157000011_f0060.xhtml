The anatomical illustration shows four views of the cervical vertebrae, atlas or C 1, and axis or C 2, with numbered labels. The top left panel shows a superior view of atlas or C 1, with labels 1 to 7. Label 1 indicates the facet for dens. Label 2 points to the anterior tubercle. Label 3 identifies the anterior arch, while label 4 shows the transverse process. Label 5 indicates the superior articular surface of the atlas, and label 6 points to the groove for the vertebral artery. Label 7 identifies the posterior arch. The bottom left panel shows an inferior view of the atlas with labels 8 to 10. Label 8 shows the transverse foramen. Label 9 indicates the anterior arch. Label 10 points to the inferior articular surface of the atlas. The bottom right panel shows a posterosuperior view of axis or C 2 with labels 11 to 13. Label 11 identifies the spinous process. Label 12 shows the superior articular facet. Label 13 indicates the dens of the axis. The top right panel shows an anterior view of the axis or C 2 with labels 14 to 16. Label 14 points to the body of the axis. Label 15 identifies the superior articular facet for the atlas. Label 16 shows the pedicle of the vertebral arch.
