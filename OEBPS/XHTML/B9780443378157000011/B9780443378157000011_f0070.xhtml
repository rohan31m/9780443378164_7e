The anatomical illustration shows the ligaments that hold the upper spine together and connect the neck bones to the skull, viewed from the back with numbered labels. The top panel, labeled upper part of vertebral canal with spinous processes and parts of vertebral arches removed to expose ligaments on posterior vertebral bodies: posterior view, has labels 1 to 5. Label 1 indicates the capsule of the atlantooccipital joint, while label 2 points to the capsule of the lateral atlantoaxial joint. Label 3 identifies the capsule of the zygapophysial joint between the axis and the C 3 vertebra, and label 4 shows the tectorial membrane of the cervical vertebral column. Label 5 indicates the posterior longitudinal ligament. The bottom panel, labeled principal part of tectorial membrane removed to expose deeper ligaments: posterior view, with labels 6 to 9. Label 6 points to the alar ligaments. Label 7 identifies the atlas or C 1. Label 8 shows the axis or C 2, while label 9 indicates the cruciform ligament, consisting of the superior longitudinal band, the transverse ligament of the atlas, and the inferior longitudinal band.
