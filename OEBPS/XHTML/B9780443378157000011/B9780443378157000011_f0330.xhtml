The anatomical illustration of the arterial blood supply to the brain displays a view of the base of the brain, with the arteries forming the cerebral arterial circle, also known as the Circle of Willis, and their branches with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the anterior communicating artery. Label 2 points to the anterior cerebral artery. Label 3 identifies the internal carotid artery. Label 4 shows the middle cerebral artery. Label 5 indicates the posterior communicating artery. Label 6 points to the posterior cerebral artery. Label 7 identifies the superior cerebellar artery. Label 8 shows the basilar artery. Label 9 indicates the anterior inferior cerebellar artery. Label 10 points to the vertebral artery. Finally, label 11 identifies the posterior inferior cerebellar artery.
