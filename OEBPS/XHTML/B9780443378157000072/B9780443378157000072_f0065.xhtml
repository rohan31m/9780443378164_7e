The anatomical illustration of the right ankle and foot ligaments presents a medial view with the ligaments and bones marked with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the medial collateral ligament of the ankle, also known as the deltoid ligament, which is made up of the posterior tibiotalar, tibiocalcaneal, tibionavicular, and anterior tibiotalar ligaments. Label 2 points to the dorsal talonavicular ligament. Label 3 shows the navicular bone. Label 4 identifies the tuberosity of the first metatarsal bone. Label 5 indicates the medial cuneiform bone. Label 6 points to the tibialis anterior tendon. Label 7 shows the plantar calcaneonavicular ligament, also called the spring ligament. Label 8 identifies the long plantar ligament. Label 9 indicates the calcaneal tendon, or Achilles tendon, which is cut in this view. Label 10 points to the posterior talocalcaneal ligament. Label 11 shows the medial talocalcaneal ligament. Label 12 identifies the tibia.
