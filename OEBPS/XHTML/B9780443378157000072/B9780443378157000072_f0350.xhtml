The superficial anterior anatomical illustration of the veins and nerves of the human lower limb presents numbered labels for the related nerves and veins. Label 1 indicates the lateral femoral cutaneous nerve. Label 2 shows the saphenous opening or fossa ovalis. Label 3 points to the anterior cutaneous branches of the femoral nerve. Label 4 identifies the branches of the lateral sural cutaneous nerve, which come from the common fibular nerve. Label 5 shows the dorsal metatarsal veins. Label 6 indicates the dorsal venous arch of the foot. Label 7 points to the great saphenous vein. Label 8 identifies the saphenous nerve, which is the terminal branch of the femoral nerve. Label 9 shows the cutaneous branches of the obturator nerve. Label 10 indicates the great saphenous vein again. Label 11 points to the femoral vein.
