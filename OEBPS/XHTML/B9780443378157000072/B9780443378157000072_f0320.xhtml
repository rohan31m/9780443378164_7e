The posterior anatomical view of the structures of the human lower limb and gluteal region presents numbered labels for the related nerves. Label 1 indicates the inferior gluteal artery and nerve. Label 2 points to the pudendal nerve, arising from spinal nerves S 2, S 3, and S 4. Label 3 shows the posterior femoral cutaneous nerve. Label 4 identifies the sciatic nerve, formed from roots L 4 to S 3. Label 5 indicates the semitendinosus muscle, which is retracted in this view. Label 6 points to the popliteal vein and artery. Label 7 shows the tibial nerve, derived from roots L 4 to S 3. Label 8 identifies the small saphenous vein. Label 9 indicates the common fibular nerve, formed from roots L 4 to S 2. Label 10 points to the semimembranosus muscle. Label 11 shows the long head of the biceps femoris muscle, which is retracted. Label 12 identifies the vastus lateralis muscle, located deep to the iliotibial tract. Label 13 indicates the piriformis muscle. Label 14 points to the superior gluteal artery and nerve.
