The posterior anatomical illustration of the human lower leg and the plantar view of the foot nerves present numbered labels for the related nerves in a posterior view of the leg and a foot. The posterior view of the leg on the left has labels 1 to 6 and 9, and labels 8, 10, 11, and 12 in the foot view, and label 7 in both views. Label 1 indicates the tibial nerve, which arises from spinal roots L 4 to L 5 and S 1 to S 3. Label 2 points to the medial sural cutaneous nerve, which is cut in this view. Label 3 shows the common fibular nerve. Label 4 identifies the lateral sural cutaneous nerve, which is also cut. Label 5 indicates the soleus muscle, which is cut and partly retracted. Label 6 points to the flexor digitorum longus muscle. Label 7 shows the tibial nerve continuing downward. Label 8 identifies the medial plantar nerve. Label 9 indicates the flexor hallucis longus muscle. Label 10 points to the lateral plantar nerve. Label 11 shows the superficial branch of the lateral plantar nerve. Label 12 identifies the common plantar digital nerves.
