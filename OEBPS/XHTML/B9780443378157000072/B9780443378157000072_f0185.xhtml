The anatomical illustration of the thigh presents the major nerves and their relationship to the hamstring muscles with numbered labels. Label 1 indicates the semimembranosus muscle. Label 2 points to the semitendinosus muscle, which is retracted in this view. Label 3 shows the gracilis muscle. Label 4 identifies the ischial tuberosity. Label 5 indicates the obturator internus muscle. Label 6 points to the vastus lateralis muscle, which lies deep to the iliotibial tract. Label 7 shows the sciatic nerve running down the posterior thigh. Label 8 identifies the common fibular nerve as it branches from the sciatic nerve. Label 9 indicates the plantaris muscle.
