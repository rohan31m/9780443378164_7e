The anatomical dissection presents a detailed inferior view of the male perineum and external genital region, with fifteen numbered structures. Number 1 identifies the distal rounded portion of the organ. Number 2 indicates the thin outer skin covering its shaft, while number 3 marks the loose connective tissue layer beneath it. Number 4 corresponds to the firm fascial sheath encasing deeper erectile structures. Number 5 highlights a muscle from the ischial bone that helps compress the erectile tissue, shown partly cut away. Number 6 denotes a narrow horizontal muscle extending across the perineal region near the midline. Number 7 marks the fibromuscular node where several pelvic floor muscles converge. Number 8 shows the circular muscle encircling the terminal part of the digestive tract. Number 9 points to the broad muscular floor of the pelvis, forming the roof of the fat-filled space on either side. Number 10 identifies the large posterior gluteal muscle forming the buttocks. Number 11 shows the bony prominence of the ischium that supports the body when seated. Number 12 indicates the terminal external opening of the digestive tract. Number 13 marks the curved part of the pelvic bone forming the boundary of the urogenital triangle. Number 14 highlights the fibrous sheet forming the structural support of the urogenital diaphragm. Finally, number 15 represents the retractable skin fold that covers the tip of the organ.
