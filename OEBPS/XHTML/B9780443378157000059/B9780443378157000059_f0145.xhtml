The anatomical illustration presents a magnified sectional view of the testis and its associated duct system, labeled from 1 to 7 to illustrate the path of sperm transport. Number 1 identifies the thick-walled tubular structure emerging from the posterior surface of the testis, responsible for conveying sperm toward the pelvic cavity. Number 2 highlights the coiled organ resting along the posterior testicular border, divided into a rounded upper part, an elongated middle part, and a tapered lower part. Number 3 marks the small compartments within the testis that contain tightly coiled seminiferous tubules where sperm formation occurs. Number 4 denotes the dense white fibrous covering that encases and protects the testicular tissue. Number 5 points to the thin connective partitions extending inward from the capsule to divide the gland into lobules. Number 6 indicates the central region where the seminiferous tubules converge into a network of channels within the mediastinum. Finally, number 7 marks the fine ducts that connect this network to the head of the adjacent coiled structure, serving as the passageway for sperm leaving the testis.
