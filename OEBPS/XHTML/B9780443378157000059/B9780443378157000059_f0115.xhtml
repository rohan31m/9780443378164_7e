This anatomical dissection depicts numbered structures 1 to 14, illustrating the continuity between the pelvic cavity and the inguinal region. Number 1 highlights a pair of slender vascular structures descending from the posterior abdominal wall toward the reproductive organ. Number 2 shows a narrow tubular structure running obliquely downward and medially across the pelvic brim. Number 3 represents an oval glandular organ located near the lateral wall of the pelvis. Number 4 traces a fibromuscular band extending anteriorly from the uterus toward the inguinal canal. Number 5 indicates the pear-shaped central reproductive organ positioned in the midline of the pelvic cavity. Number 6 depicts a hollow muscular organ lying anterior and inferior to the uterus. Number 7 identifies an external fold of skin forming part of the outer genital region. Number 8 points to the terminal extensions of a fibrous cord near the superficial opening of the inguinal canal. Number 9 corresponds to a small branch of a mixed nerve providing sensory supply to the genital area. Number 10 marks an oval external opening in the anterior abdominal wall. Number 11 refers to a pair of major vessels passing beneath the inguinal ligament into the thigh. Number 12 denotes a deep internal aperture through which structures pass from the abdominal cavity into the inguinal canal. Number 13 shows the thin fascial layer forming the inner lining of the abdominal wall. Finally, number 14 represents the fibrous band stretching between the anterior superior iliac spine and the pubic tubercle, forming the lower boundary of the abdomen.
