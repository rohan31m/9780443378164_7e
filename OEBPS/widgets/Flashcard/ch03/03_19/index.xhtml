Topography of Lungs: Anterior View

Comment

Lungs lie adjacent to the parietal pleura inferiorly to the sixth costal cartilage. Beyond this point, the lungs do not occupy the full extent of the pleural cavity during quiet respiration. At the midclavicular line, the margin of the lung is the 6th rib, at the midaxillary line it is the 8th rib, and posteriorly at the paravertebral line the lung margin is at the 10th rib. In quiet respiration, the surface landmarks of the pleural margins however extend two ribs inferior to the lung margins.

Clinical

It is important for physicians to be able to “visualize” the extent of the lungs and pleural cavities topographically on the chest of their patients, especially since physicians often need to auscultate (listen to lung sounds with a stethoscope) the lungs. Additionally, they also need to percuss the lungs.
