Hepatic Portal Vein Tributaries: Portacaval Anastomoses

Comment: The hepatic portal vein is usually formed by the union of the splenic vein and superior mesenteric vein (SMV). However, the inferior mesenteric vein sometimes joins the SMV or drains into the site where the SMV and splenic veins meet.

Clinical: Important portacaval anastomotic sites include sites around the esophagus, the paraumbilical region, the rectum, and where portions of the gastrointestinal tract are in a retroperitoneal position.

If portal blood flow is decreased or prevented from flowing through the liver, the venous blood still can reach the heart via these important portacaval anastomoses. Likewise, if the inferior vena cava is partially compressed or obstructed, venous blood can flow via the portacaval anastomoses into the portal system of veins.
