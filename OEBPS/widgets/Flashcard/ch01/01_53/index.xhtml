Nerves of Oral Cavity

Comment

Taste of the posterior third of the tongue is largely via the glossopharyngeal nerve, while taste to the anterior two-thirds is via the facial nerve (chorda tympani). General sensation of the epiglottis is via CN X, whereas most of the posterior tongue is via CN IX, and general sensation to the anterior two-thirds is by CN V3 (lingual nerve). Some overlap is common.

Clinical

Tooth decay (dental caries) can lead to cavities, which are caused by bacteria that convert food debris into acids that form plaque that adheres to the teeth. These acids can erode into the enamel and form a cavity. Enamel is an acellular material and is the hardest substance in the body! As adults age, periodontal disease accounts for a significant loss of teeth but can be prevented if diagnosed early enough.
