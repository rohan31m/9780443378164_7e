Fascia of Neck

Comment: The superficial investing cervical fascia lies beneath the dermis of the skin and above an underlying layer of deep investing cervical fascia. It contains sweat glands, blood and lymphatic vessels, superficial lymph nodes, cutaneous nerves, and variable amounts of fat. The deep investing cervical fascia is thinner, does not contain fat, and includes three layers: investing cervical, pretracheal, and prevertebral. The investing layer lies deep into the skin, surrounds the neck, and divides to enclose the sternocleidomastoid and trapezius muscles. The pretracheal layer is in the anterior neck and encloses several infrahyoid muscles and the thyroid gland, trachea, and esophagus. Posterosuperiorly, it is called the buccopharyngeal fascia because it covers the buccinator and pharyngeal constrictor muscles and blends with the carotid sheaths. The prevertebral layer, as its name implies, surrounds the vertebral column and its associated muscles.

Clinical:Fascial layers provide muscular compartments, can limit the spread of infections, hold muscles and tendons in place, and form bursae (contain synovial fluid) at joints to reduce friction when flexed or extended.
