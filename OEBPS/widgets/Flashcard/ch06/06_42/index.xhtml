Forearm Muscles

Origin (proximal): Extensor pollicis brevis muscle arises from the posterior surface of the radius and the interosseous membrane.

Insertion (distal): Attaches to the dorsal base of the proximal phalanx of the thumb.

Action: Extends the proximal phalanx of the thumb at the metacarpophalangeal joint. By its continued action, it also can extend the 1st metacarpal bone at the carpometacarpal joint.

Innervation: Radial nerve (posterior interosseous branch) (C7 and C8).

Comment: The extensor pollicis brevis muscle, a short extensor of the thumb, courses with the abductor pollicis longus muscle. The tendons of the two muscles form part of the lateral boundary of the “anatomical snuffbox.” The radial artery pulse can be felt in the snuffbox.

Clinical: One can test the extensor pollicis brevis muscle clinically by having the patient supinate the extended hand and then attempt to extend the thumb (the hitchhiking thumb position) against resistance. Not only will the strength of the muscle be tested, but the tendon of the muscle should be visible and palpable over the dorsal surface of the thumb’s metacarpophalangeal joint.
