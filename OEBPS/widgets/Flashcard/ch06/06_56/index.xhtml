Cross Section of Palm

Comment

Muscles at the base of the thumb compose the thenar cone, or eminence.

Intrinsic muscles at the base of the fifth digit make up the hypothenar eminence.

The palmar interosseous muscles adduct (PAD) the middle three fingers, whereas the dorsal interosseous muscles abduct (DAB) the middle three fingers. Together, the interossei also flex the metacarpophalangeal joint and, because of their insertion into the extensor expansion, extend the proximal and distal interphalangeal joints.

Clinical

Several potential spaces exist in the palm and can become sites of infection. The thenar space exists just anterior to the adductor pollicis muscle. The midpalmar space exists posterior (deep) to the central compartment that contains the long flexor tendons and lumbrical muscles.

Infections in the fifth digit may “seed” the common flexor sheath, and vice versa, via this connection.
