Forearm Muscles

Origin (proximal): Extensor digitorum muscle arises from lateral epicondyle of the humerus.

Insertion (distal): Extensor digitorum muscles insert as extensor expansions of the medial four digits (index finger to little finger).

Action: Extensor digitorum muscle performs extension at the metacarpophalangeal and interphalangeal joints. This muscle also participates in wrist extension when the fingers are extended.

Innervation: Radial nerve (posterior interosseous branch) (C7 and C8).

Comment: The tendons of the extensor digitorum pass through the extensor retinaculum and to the extensor expansions of the medial four digits. Occasionally, this muscle has only three tendons instead of four. Often, the extensor digiti minimi is attached to the extensor digitorum.

Clinical: The extensor digitorum muscle is tested clinically by having the patient extend the fingers against resistance. This is best done as one supports the patient’s pronated hand with one hand and provides resistance to the fingers across the metacarpo-phalangeal joints with the other hand. The contracting muscle belly will bulge out along the lateral forearm (little finger side in the pronated position).
