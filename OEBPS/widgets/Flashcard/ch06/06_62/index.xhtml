Cutaneous Innervation of Upper Limb: Posterior View

Comment

These cutaneous nerves reside in the subcutaneous tissue and supply the overlying skin of the upper limb. These nerves correspond to the outflow from the lower cervical plexus (clavicular region, C3–C4) and the entire brachial plexus (C5–T1). They distribute from the lateral and anterior arm and forearm dermatomes (C5–C6) to the palmar aspect of the hand dermatomes (C6–C8), and then course up the medial aspect of the forearm (C8) and arm dermatomes (C8–T1, T2). These dermatome areas are approximations because they vary from person to person and often overlap, but they provide a general distribution of the cutaneous innervation. One can test median nerve neuropathy on the hand by two-point discrimination on the palmar index finger. The ulna nerve can be tested by digital compression on the distal medial wrist (affects ring and little finger). The radial nerve can be tested by measuring the grip strength of the hand (see Cards 6-61 and 6-62).
