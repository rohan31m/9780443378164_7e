Pelvic Diaphragm: Female

Origin

Coccygeus muscle arises from the spine of the ischium and the sacrospinous ligament.

Insertion

Attaches to the coccyx and lower portion of the sacrum.

Action

With the levator ani, the coccygeus supports the pelvic floor. It also draws the coccyx forward after the coccyx has been pushed back during parturition (in females) or defecation.

Innervation

Anterior rami of S4 and S5.

Comment

The pelvic diaphragm comprises the coccygeus and levator ani. Together, these muscles support and raise the pelvic floor.

The coccygeus muscle is the muscle used by dogs to tuck their tails between their hind legs; in humans, it is largely a mixture of skeletal muscle fibers and fibrous connective tissue.

Clinical

Assisting the levator ani, the coccygeus pulls the coccyx forward after defecation and forms the posterior portion of the pelvic diaphragm.
