Female Endopelvic Fascia and Contents

Comment: Deep within the female pelvis one sees peritoneal recesses between the urinary bladder and uterus (vesicouterine pouch), and a recess between the rectum and uterus called the rectouterine pouch (of Douglas). The uterus is also supported by the cardinal or transverse cervical (Mackenrodt’s) ligaments, and by the uterosacral ligaments.

Clinical: Uterine prolapse involves the loss of support by cardinal and uterosacral ligaments and the pelvic diaphragm (levator ani portion). When these important support structures are weakened, the broad ligament alone is not sufficient to support the uterus or prevent its prolapse.

The uterus may descend slightly (common in parous women) or may reach the vaginal orifice or beyond (complete prolapse).
