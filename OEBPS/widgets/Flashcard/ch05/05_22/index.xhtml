Female Inguinal Canal and Round Ligament

Comment

The uterus is a pear-shaped muscular organ which, in the adult, is anteverted and anteflexed relative to the cervix, with its body lying over the urinary bladder (see Card 5-13/S4-33). The broad ligament, a double layer of peritoneum, envelops the ovaries, uterine tubes, and the uterus (see Cards 5-19/S7-4 and 5-20/S7-5).

The ovarian ligament attaches the ovary to the lateral wall of the uterus and then reflects off the uterus anteriorly and laterally as the round ligament of the uterus. It then enters the deep inguinal ring and terminates in the labium majora.

Clinical

The anterior aspect of the vulva (mons pubis and anterior labia) has a rich supply of lymphatic vessels draining the vaginal orifice and vestibule.
