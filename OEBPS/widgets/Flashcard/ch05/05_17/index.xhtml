Prostatic Venous Plexus

Comment

The prostatic artery, supplying the prostate gland, is a branch of the inferior vesical artery (the artery to the urinary bladder). Significant variability in the arteries to the pelvic viscera can exist, and the arteries often form small anastomotic connections. Extensive venous plexuses are associated with the bladder, rectum, and prostate in the male.
