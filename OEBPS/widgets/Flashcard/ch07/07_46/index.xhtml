Muscles of Leg: Posterior View

Origin (proximal)

Plantaris muscle arises from the inferior end of the lateral supracondylar line of the femur and oblique popliteal ligament.

Insertion (distal)

Plantaris muscle’s long, slender tendon crosses obliquely between the gastrocnemius and soleus and inserts into the posterior part of the calcaneus, often fusing with the calcaneal tendon.

Action

Plantaris muscle weakly assists the gastrocnemius in plantarflexion of the foot at the ankle and flexion of the leg at the knee.

Comment

The gastrocnemius, soleus, and plantaris muscles form the superficial group of muscles in the posterior compartment of the leg. The tibial nerve and posterior tibial vessels run just deep to these three muscles.

Clinical

The plantaris muscle is absent in a small proportion of the population (5% to 10%) and is vestigial in humans. The small tendon may be used for grafting, especially in reconstructive surgery of the hand, where tendons may have been damaged beyond repair.
