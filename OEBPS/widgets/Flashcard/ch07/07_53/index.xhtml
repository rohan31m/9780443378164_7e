Muscles of Sole of Foot: First Layer

Origin (proximal)

Flexor digitorum brevis muscle arises from the medial tubercle of the tuberosity of the calcaneus and from the plantar aponeurosis and intermuscular septa.

Insertion (distal)

The flexor digitorum brevis muscle gives rise to four tendons that are superficial to the tendons of the flexor digitorum longus. Within their digital tendon sheaths, the tendons of the brevis split to allow the long flexor tendons to pass to the distal phalanges. The brevis tendons insert on both sides of the middle phalanges of the four lateral toes.

Action

Flexor digitorum brevis muscle flexes the second (middle) phalanx of the four lateral toes.

Comment

The arrangement of the flexor digitorum longus and brevis tendons in the foot is similar to that of the superficial and deep digital flexors in the hand.

Clinical

Generally, unlike the muscles of the hand, the muscles of the sole of the foot act as a unit to maintain balance, maintain the arches of the foot (along with the supporting ligaments), and act in pushing the foot off the ground.
