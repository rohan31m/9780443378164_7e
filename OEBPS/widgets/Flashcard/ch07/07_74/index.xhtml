Arteries of Sole of Foot

Comment

The medial and lateral plantar arteries are continuations of the posterior tibial artery.

The lateral plantar artery is much larger than the medial branch. It forms the major portion of the plantar arch, which anastomoses with other plantar branches and the dorsalis pedis artery.

Plantar metatarsal arteries arise from this plantar arch and give rise to proper plantar digital branches. The foot also contains superficial and deep veins. The superficial veins are subcutaneous and drain most of the blood from the foot; they do not possess corresponding arteries. However, the deep veins do accompany the arteries. These plantar and dorsal veins then drain into the small and great saphenous veins.

Clinical

Puncture wounds or lacerations to the sole of the foot may bleed profusely because of the rich vascular anastomoses of the plantar arches. Moreover, because of the tight, deep compartments containing tendons, muscles, and ligaments in the sole, controlling the bleeding from a laceration may be problematic.
